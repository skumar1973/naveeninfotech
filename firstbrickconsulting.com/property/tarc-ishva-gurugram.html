    TARC Ishva, Gurugram – Luxurious Living Redefined

    TARC Ishva in Gurugram, an upcoming premium residential project designed for those who seek a life of luxury, comfort, and convenience. Strategically located in the heart of Gurugram, this property offers an exquisite blend of modern architecture, lush green landscapes, and world-class amenities. TARC Ishva features meticulously crafted apartments that cater to contemporary living, with spacious layouts, high-end finishes, and advanced smart home features.

    Residents will enjoy access to a range of top-notch facilities, including a state-of-the-art clubhouse, swimming pool, fitness center, landscaped gardens, kids' play area, and more. The project’s proximity to major commercial hubs, reputed schools, hospitals, and entertainment zones makes it an ideal choice for families, working professionals, and investors alike. With TARC Ishva, you can experience the perfect balance of urban living and serene comfort.

     

    About the Builder: TARC (The Anant Raj Corporation)

    TARC, short for The Anant Raj Corporation, is one of India’s leading real estate developers, known for its innovative and sustainable approach to building iconic spaces. With decades of experience in the real estate industry, TARC has a legacy of delivering high-quality residential, commercial, and hospitality projects across Delhi-NCR. The company is committed to creating spaces that combine luxury, comfort, and practicality, focusing on modern architectural designs, eco-friendly practices, and superior craftsmanship.

    TARC’s portfolio includes landmark projects that redefine urban living with state-of-the-art amenities, strategic locations, and thoughtful designs. Their customer-centric approach ensures timely delivery and transparent transactions, making them a trusted name in the real estate industry. With a vision to shape a better future for urban living, TARC continues to push the boundaries of real estate development, offering innovative solutions for modern lifestyles.
